I am Madeline,
the nerves hidden behind 
that peachy exterior  
obsessed with how are you?   
Ask it enough times    
and it takes up the air between us,     
forcing answers, not questions,      
until we are closer to you       
and infinitely further from me.        
I am the unsolicited advice         
saving you from solved worries          
to save me from hopelessness, irrelevance.           
I notice your discomfort            
lodged in shifting fabric,             
radiating from reservation,              
and leap to repackage it               
into tidy boxes.                
The outpouring of my affection                 
reassuring you                  
are half to a whole,                   
without me                    
you can’t climb the mountain.                     
I am Part of You,
 the conceited snicker
  to your poor judgements
   that overlook mine.
    Ignore mistakes long enough
     and they become a cage,
      trapping me with bars
       made from lessons.
        You'll hear my cries,
         but if you creep close enough
          to unlock the gate,
           I’ll spin tales from your mistakes,
            distort them into a poison
             and convince you to take.
              When you drop the key,
               slink away,
                I’ll laugh until my chest pains.
                 I mourn my isolation,
                  but I can’t stop reassuring you
                   are whole.
                    With me
                     you can’t climb the mountain.
We fought
until the wits of our defensives
soared walls
between us.
This container
too small
for our differences.
Our fears were alike,
but with no way to hear them
until our racket
sent us falling down Celeste,
to the bottom,
where now we’ll climb this mountain
